The Roots of Progress Blog-Building Intensive is a 10-week online fellowship program for writers passionate about progress studies. Now in its fourth year, the program helps writers refine their ideas, improve their craft, and build audiences for long-form essays about progress, abundance policy, and related topics.

## 2026 Fellowship

**Program dates:** July 27 – October 2, 2026  
**Application deadline:** June 1, 2026 (decisions by July 7)  
**Cost:** Free (includes retreat accommodations and Progress Conference ticket)

[Apply now](https://rootsofprogress.typeform.com/to/zOB6TqEY)

## What Fellows Do

### Write Four Essays

Over four two-week cycles, fellows produce:
- Three essays of approximately 1,000 words each
- One research essay of 2,000–5,000 words

### Learn from Professionals

Teaching staff includes:
- **Emma McAleavy** (Program Manager): Previously published in *New York Times*, *Boston Globe*, *Outside*
- **Mike Riggs** (Developmental Editor): Former deputy managing editor at *Reason* magazine

### Build Community

Fellows join approximately 35 peers for twice-weekly group sessions, brainstorming, and editing exchanges. An in-person retreat takes place August 20-23 in Pennsylvania. Alumni remain connected through ongoing Slack channels and collaborative feedback.

### Meet Progress Leaders

Fellows engage with prominent intellectuals and advisors including Tyler Cowen, Greg Lukianoff, Alice Evans, Kevin Esvelt, Eli Dourado, Virginia Postrel, Brian Potter, and others.

## Time Commitment

**10-15 hours weekly** for 11 weeks:
- Live sessions: 3-5 hours
- Research/writing: 4-6 hours
- Peer editing: 1-2 hours
- Background reading: 1-5 hours

Sessions are scheduled across time zones (mornings Pacific, midday Eastern, evenings European).

## Who Should Apply

Ideal candidates:
- Demonstrate strong, clear writing ability
- Are aligned with progress studies concepts (human agency, solutionism, technological optimism)
- Are committed to writing regularly on progress topics
- Can dedicate 10-15 weekly hours for 11 weeks

## FAQ

**Is this program free?**  
Yes. The program is sponsored by RPI donors. All retreat costs, meals, and conference attendance are covered.

**Online or in-person?**  
Primarily online with a required three-day in-person retreat and optional Progress Conference attendance.

**What knowledge of progress studies is needed?**  
Core alignment with progress ideas; RPI provides learning resources for those newer to the field.
