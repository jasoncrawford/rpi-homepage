*The Techno-Humanist Manifesto* is a forthcoming book by Jason Crawford, founder and president of the Roots of Progress Institute, outlining a contemporary philosophy of progress.

## What is Techno-Humanism?

Techno-humanism is the view that science, technology, and industry are good—not in themselves, but because they ultimately promote human well-being and flourishing. The underlying premise is that material advancement serves as a foundation for broader human progress.

## Structure of the Manifesto

The book comprises three major sections:

**Part 1: The Value of Progress**  
Why advancement matters—from appreciating industrial civilization to understanding human agency, well-being, and active problem-solving.

**Part 2: The Future of Progress**  
Acceleration patterns, resource limitations, and humanity's next evolutionary phase beyond agriculture and industry.

**Part 3: A Culture of Progress**  
Lost optimism, establishing new moral ideals and heroic archetypes for the progress movement.

## Purpose

The manifesto aims to:
- Provide moral justification for material progress
- Establish a conceptual framework for the progress movement
- Present an aspirational vision for the future
- Address progress-related challenges constructively
- Elevate progress to both a practical and moral imperative

## For Whom

The work targets scientists, engineers, entrepreneurs, intellectuals, and policymakers seeking to understand progress as both practical endeavor and moral imperative.
