Progress in Medicine is a summer career exploration program for high school students interested in medicine, biotechnology, health policy, and longevity fields.

> "People today live longer, healthier, and less painful lives than ever before. Why? Who made those changes possible?"

## 2026 Program

**Online dates:** June 15 – July 10 & July 20 – 24, 2026  
**Residential:** July 15–19, 2026 at Stanford University, Palo Alto, CA  
**Cost:** $2,000 (scholarships available)

Applications for 2026 are now closed. Check back in November 2026 for the 2027 program announcement.

---

## What Students Do

### Five Weeks Online

Live instruction 1–3 PM PT / 4–6 PM ET, Monday–Thursday (2 hours/day). Friday independent work: reading, reflection, writing. The curriculum explores:

- History of medical progress through case studies
- Career frameworks and pathways in health/biotech fields
- Public health origins, infectious diseases, cancer biology
- Medical research ethics and regulations
- Emerging frontiers in medicine and longevity

### One Week at Stanford

Students live in Stanford dorms (double occupancy) and tour:
- Snyder Lab (genetics and disease research)
- Goodman Surgical Education Center
- Multiply Labs (biomanufacturing robots)
- Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research (SIEPR)

### The 10-Hour Essay

The final week focuses on a personal narrative writing project, helping students communicate their interests and goals in medicine.

## Eligibility

- Rising sophomores to rising seniors (ages 15–17)
- GPA 3.5+ or demonstrated intellectual achievement
- Able to commit to 10+ hours/week for 6 weeks
- Can attend all four live online sessions weekly
- Can travel independently to California (age 16+) or have parental support

*No letters of recommendation, transcripts, or standardized test scores required during initial application.*

---

## After the Program

Program completion includes:
- Certificate of completion
- Personalized written assessment
- Ongoing access to online course platform and discussion boards
- Select participants eligible for letters of recommendation, writing feedback, or conference invitations
