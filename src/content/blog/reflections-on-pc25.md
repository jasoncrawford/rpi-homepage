The second annual Progress Conference concluded with outstanding results, drawing over 350 participants including builders, academics, policymakers, investors, and writers committed to human advancement.

## Conference Success Metrics

Attendees rated the experience exceptionally highly, with an average score of 9.3/10 and a net promoter score of +83. One participant captured the sentiment: "This is the best conference I have ever been to. The attendees quality, the venue and the operations details are top notch."

The 2025 gathering expanded significantly from its 2024 predecessor, extending to more days and accommodating a larger audience. The event sold out five months before taking place, with hundreds of qualified applicants unable to secure spots.

## Core Achievements

**Community Building:** Attendees consistently emphasized the caliber of connections made. One noted experiencing "2 days of living in the future" while meeting frontier innovators across diverse fields.

**Idea Generation and Catalysis:** Participants reported generating dozens of ideas and professional contacts expected to yield long-term benefits.

**Cultural Foundation:** The gathering fostered what observers described as "openness, honest discussions, and friendliness" within a community of serious thinkers.

## Knowledge Sharing

Conference talks are being released on the RPI YouTube channel, with two plenary interviews already available featuring Sam Altman and Blake Scholl in conversation with Tyler Cowen.

Multiple attendees published reflections inspired by the conference, collected on the RPI Notion page. Additionally, Big Think launched a special issue titled "The Engine of Progress," featuring essays from conference participants and RPI fellows exploring human advancement.

## Looking Forward

**Save the date:** Progress Conference 2026 will convene October 8-11 at Lighthaven in Berkeley, California. Organizers are exploring expanded programming to accommodate more participants and smaller specialized events addressing specific challenges.
