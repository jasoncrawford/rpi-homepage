The Roots of Progress Institute has announced its second cohort of fellows for the Blog-Building Intensive Fellowship program. This year's class consists of 25 talented writers and thinkers focused on progress studies, selected from over 350 applicants.

The fellows represent diverse professional backgrounds—founders, researchers, academics, policy-makers, capital allocators, and journalists—drawn from across North America, Europe, and Hong Kong. Their writing topics span nuclear fusion, reproductive longevity, space manufacturing, housing reform, urban mobility, industrial policy, AI safety, biotechnology, and eco-modernism.

## Program Structure

The eight-week intensive begins August 22nd and includes structured onboarding. During the program, participants will "meet and learn from seventeen expert program advisors" and engage in "bi-weekly structured writing gyms" with professional editors providing feedback. Fellows will complete four two-week writing sprints and publish four essays while committing 10-15 hours weekly.

The institute expanded from selecting 15-20 fellows to 25 this year, reflecting increased applicant quality. The organization emphasizes that "the tide of history isn't always carried by the side with the best ideas," but rather by those most effective at advocating them intellectually.
