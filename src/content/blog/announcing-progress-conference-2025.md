The Roots of Progress Institute is announcing the second annual Progress Conference, scheduled for October 16-19, 2025, in Berkeley, California. Building on last year's successful inaugural event that drew 200 attendees, this year's conference will expand to include "300+ builders, storytellers, policy makers, intellectuals, and students."

## Key Details

The event is being co-hosted by eight organizations including Abundance Institute, Foresight Institute, and the Institute for Progress. The conference features keynote speakers including Sam Altman, Tyler Cowen, Jennifer Pahlka, and Blake Scholl, with over 30 additional speakers across four tracks: AI protopia, health/biotech/longevity, policy, and American Dynamism.

## Format

The format extends beyond the main two-day conference, with Thursday and Sunday serving as optional add-on days featuring "interest groups and other activities, such as SF Bay Area company tours." Attendees can participate in unconference sessions and pitch projects to potential collaborators.

The conference operates on an invitation basis, though the institute is accepting applications through May 15th. Early sponsors include Open Philanthropy, Astera Institute, and Freethink, with additional sponsorship opportunities available.
