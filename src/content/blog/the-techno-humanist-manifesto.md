Roots of Progress has announced *The Techno-Humanist Manifesto*, a forthcoming book by founder and president Jason Crawford outlining a contemporary philosophy of progress.

## Core Philosophy

The work introduces "techno-humanism," described as "the view that science, technology, and industry are good—not in themselves, but because they ultimately promote human well-being and flourishing." The underlying premise posits that material advancement serves as a foundation for broader human progress.

## Book Purpose

Crawford intends the manifesto to accomplish several goals:
- Provide moral justification for material progress
- Establish a conceptual framework for the progress movement
- Present an aspirational vision for the future
- Address progress-related challenges constructively
- Elevate progress to both a practical and moral imperative

## Intended Audience

The work targets scientists, engineers, and founders seeking to understand their contributions' moral significance, alongside intellectuals, policymakers, and anyone engaged with progress-oriented thinking.

## Publication Plan

The book will be serialized essay-by-essay across Jason Crawford's blog, the Roots of Progress Substack, and Freethink Media's Voices feature. Readers can access the work through a Substack subscription, with paid options supporting the project.
