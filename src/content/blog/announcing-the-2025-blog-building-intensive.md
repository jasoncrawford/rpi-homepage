The Roots of Progress Institute has opened applications for its 2025 Blog-Building Intensive, a 10-week fellowship program designed for aspiring writers interested in progress studies.

## Program Overview

The initiative welcomes participants to "launch (or re-launch) a blog/Substack, get into a regular writing habit, improve your writing, and make progress on building your audience." Fellows will produce four essays over the course of the program, with feedback from professional editors and peers.

## 2024 Fellow Achievements

Last year's 24 fellows (selected from over 300 applicants) generated substantial impact. Participants published 81 essays collectively and have since contributed to major publications and policy discussions. One fellow's work earned "the top spot on the Hacker News forum," while others have been "cited in the Financial Times" and "leading the AI policy debate."

## Specialized Tracks

This year's cohort features dedicated programming around two themes: agriculture, and health/biotech/longevity. The program welcomes submissions across all progress-related topics.

## Learning Opportunities

Fellows will engage with prominent advisors including Tyler Cowen, Noah Smith, and Virginia Postrel, alongside industry experts in agriculture and biotechnology.

## Commitment & Timeline

The program requires 10–15 hours weekly for 10 weeks, runs July 28–October 10, and includes an in-person component during the Progress Conference (October 16–19). There is no program cost. Applications close June 1st.
