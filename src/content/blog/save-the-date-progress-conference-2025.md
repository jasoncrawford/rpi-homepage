A four-day event to connect people in the progress movement: Berkeley Oct 16-19

Last year the Roots of Progress Institute hosted its inaugural conference, drawing 200 attendees to Berkeley for two days of "deep conversation, catalyze new projects" and networking around human progress. The event received widespread acclaim, with many describing it as among the finest conferences they'd experienced.

Building on this success, organizers are expanding the 2025 edition to run October 16-19 in Berkeley at the Lighthaven Campus. The conference will feature prominent speakers—past participants included Patrick Collison, Tyler Cowen, and Steven Pinker—along with unconference sessions, pitch opportunities, and networking activities.

## New in 2025

The four-day format introduces new elements: Thursday and Sunday will offer optional add-on programming including "interest groups and other activities, such as SF Bay Area company tours." The main conference runs Friday and Saturday, mirroring the 2024 structure.

Attendance is expected to exceed 300 participants drawn from builders, policymakers, intellectuals, and students. While invitation-only, the organization will open applications for attendees in April, with speaker announcements arriving by the same month.

The Institute is also expanding its team, hiring Ben Thomas as Event Manager. Thomas brings operations experience from McKinsey and BibleProject, and will oversee conference planning and additional community-building initiatives for the progress movement.
