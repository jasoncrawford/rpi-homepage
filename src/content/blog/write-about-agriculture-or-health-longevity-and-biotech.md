The Roots of Progress Institute announced the opening of applications for its third Blog-Building Intensive fellowship cohort, introducing two specialized tracks focused on agriculture and health/longevity/biotech.

## Fellowship Overview

The organization seeks 5-7 fellows per themed track who possess both passion and relevant expertise. Participants commit to attending all advisor sessions for their chosen track and focusing their writing on how their topic relates to and enables progress.

## Agriculture Track

This pathway explores topics including climate adaptation strategies, precision agriculture technology adoption, cultured meat development, land use philosophy (sparing versus sharing), and regulatory approaches to food safety. The program emphasizes diverse backgrounds, from agronomists to agricultural journalists.

Agriculture advisors include Brad Zamft, Sledge Taylor, Tim Hammerich, Robert Yaman, and Alison Van Eenennaam.

## Health, Longevity & Biotech Track

This track examines longevity research importance, AI applications in drug discovery, regulatory reform for faster therapeutic access, medical history lessons, and prevention versus treatment approaches in healthcare systems.

Health advisors comprise Ruxandra Teslo, Sam Rodrigues, Niko McCarty, Saloni Dattani, Meri Beckwith, and John Willbanks.

## Program Benefits

Fellows receive expert advisor mentorship, structured writing instruction, opportunities to complete four essays, and community connection with progress-oriented peers. The fellowship remains open to broader progress-related writing with preference given to themed track applicants.
