The Roots of Progress Institute has unveiled a new summer initiative designed to inspire talented high school students to pursue careers in medicine and related fields.

## Program Overview

"Progress in Medicine" invites students to explore careers in medicine, biotech, health policy, and longevity through an innovative hybrid format. The program centers on a guiding question: "People today live longer, healthier, and less painful lives than ever before. Why? Who made those changes possible?"

## Core Components

Students will engage with historical narratives of medical breakthroughs, meet working professionals and near-peer mentors, and develop hands-on skills. The program includes a four-day residential component at Stanford University featuring lab tours and biotech company visits in the Bay Area.

## Structure & Timeline

The six-week program combines five weeks of online instruction (2 hours daily, four days weekly, June 15–July 10 and July 20–24) with an in-person residential week at Stanford (July 15–19). Cost is $2,000 with scholarships available.

## Eligibility

Current high school freshmen, sophomores, and juniors demonstrating strong academic capability and genuine interest in medicine-related careers may apply. Selection occurs through written application and Zoom interviews.

## Expert Involvement

Advisors include Celine Halioua (Loyal CEO), Amesh Adalja (Johns Hopkins), and Jared Seehafer (FDA), along with near-peer mentors working across clinical and research settings.

**Application deadline:** February 8, 2026
