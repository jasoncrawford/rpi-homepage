The Roots of Progress Institute completed its second cohort of 24 fellows through an eight-week blog-building intensive program this fall. The initiative proved successful, with participants gaining prominence in progress studies circles.

## Program Outcomes

Fellows published 81 essays collectively on diverse topics including urban development, international trade, artificial intelligence, environmental policy, and scientific progress. Several achieved notable recognition, including contributions to Time magazine's AI policy coverage, biotechnology writing recognition, Financial Times citations, and Hacker News rankings.

## Quantified Results

- Over 60% of participants rated acceleration of their progress intellectual careers at 8/10 or higher
- Seven fellows launched their first online writing presence during the program
- Existing bloggers experienced a median audience growth of 84%
- Eighty percent indicated plans to maintain peer connections post-program

## Fellow Feedback

Participants highlighted three primary benefits:

**Elevated Ambition:** Fellows credited advisor sessions and peer exposure with raising their professional standards and sense of purpose.

**Enhanced Writing:** The program's structure—including workshops, professional editing, and Rob Tracinski's writing course—helped participants develop their craft and voice.

**Community:** Participants valued the supportive network most significantly, experiencing newfound belonging within the progress movement. One fellow noted feeling "more part of the progress community" after participation.

The institute plans to announce 2025 program details in Q1 2025.
