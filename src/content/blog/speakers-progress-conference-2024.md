Progress Conference 2024 is becoming a major gathering for the progress community. The event has announced five additional speakers: Jason Carman (documentary filmmaker at S3), Celine Halioua (CEO at Loyal), Cate Hall (CEO at Astera), Lynne Kiesling (Northwestern professor and transactive energy specialist), and Hannu Rajaniemi (biotech founder at HelixNano and science fiction author).

The speaker roster is now complete, though the conference will feature "unconference spots" where attendees can organize additional sessions themselves.

## New Sponsors

Four new sponsors have joined the event: Future of Life Institute, Institute for Humane Studies, Manifold, and the Good Science Project. Additional sponsorship opportunities remain available at various funding levels ranging from $10,000 to $75,000.

## Application Update

The application process closed after receiving over 300 submissions. Despite the strong interest, organizers anticipate capacity will allow only 20–30 attendees from the applicant pool, even though they wished to accommodate approximately 100 candidates based on application quality.

The conference will convene 25 speakers and roughly 200 attendees described as "high-caliber," including intellectuals, builders, policymakers, and progress scholars.
